## Thoughts on large language models (LLMs)

I mainly use tools like **ChatGPT**, and I see it as helpful tool but not something to depend on.

LLMs can be useful for:
- Understanding concepts more clearly  
- Speeding up repetitive tasks  
- Debugging code when something is not working  

However, they are still limited, especially in how they interpret questions. The output you get depends heavily on how you ask the question, so they should be used carefully. I believe LLMs should support learninng but you shouldn't become dependent on them. They should help you become more competent. 

It is also important to be careful about:
- Biases in generated outputs  
- Accuracy of information  
- Lack of regulation  
- Overreliance on generated content  

I do think LLMs have a role in modern science, especially for helping people learn tools and concepts, but again, they should be used thoughtfully and responsibly.
